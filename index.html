How it works

Invite friends

Invite friends, family, or coworkers! Escape My Room is best experienced as part of a team.. Each escape room can hold a maximum of 7 guests, but you can book as many or as few tickets as you like. If you don’t book all 7 tickets in your timeslot, there’s a good chance someone else will book the remaining tickets and you’ll make new friends!

Buy tickets

Buy tickets here on our website. All Escape My Room tickets must be purchased at least one hour in advance. Walk-ins are not allowed. Choose your room, select the amount of tickets you’ll need, and then find a date and a time that work well for you.

Get locked in a room

Get locked in a room! You have one hour to escape, but if you do not escape within the hour, then we’ll come in and show you what you missed. Please arrive at least 15 minutes early to have time to look around the family museum. You risk forfeiting your ticket if you are more than 15 minutes late.

Solve puzzles

While in the room, you must work together as a team to find clues and solve puzzles,uncovering secrets and scandals of the DeLaporte family along the way.

Come back

Come back! We debut a new escape room every two to four months, so If you have a good time (and we promise you that you will), come back and play a different room!

Rooms

Escape My Room features rooms inspired by the DeLaporte mansion which was torn down to make way for a new hospital complex near the Superdome. Before the house was demolished, everything within it was removed and placed into storage.

In each escape room you will uncover mysteries involving the secretive DeLaporte family and their hidden role in shaping the history of New Orleans.

Mardi Gras Study

The year is 1990, and Odette DeLaporte has invited you to her study. As the last remaining heir in New Orleans to the DeLaporte fortune, she needs your help locating a treasure which went missing a long time ago.

The reclusive matriarch has not been seen in public for quite some time, yet she has agreed to meet with you with the hope that you can recover what’s been lost.

Jazz Music Parlor

Travel back in time to the scene of a murder in the music parlor of the DeLaporte Mansion. Join forces with the police and grieving DeLaporte family members as they comb the crime scene for evidence. Can the two groups cooperate long enough to solve this 60-year-old crime, or will the answers be lost forever?

The Jazz Parlor features relics of New Orleans’ most bizarre family, and numerous secrets untold. Expose the DeLaporte scandal as you learn the history of jazz and Mardi Gras Indians.

Buy Tickets

Use the calendar to purchase tickets, or open the calendar in a new window. Tickets cost $28 per person, and there are 7 tickets available for each time slot. If you would like more than one ticket, enter the number of tickets you would like to purchase before selecting a date.

If you have a code to redeem, please enter it on the first screen by clicking the button “redeem code”. Codes are given out when gift certificates are purchased. We do not currently offer any discounts on admission.

Location

Escape My Room is located in the U.S. Postal Service building on Loyola Avenue, between Girod and Julia Streets, one block from the Superdome and on streetcar line. Our building is within walking distance or a short cab ride from most major hotels. Specific details about how to enter the building will be provided upon booking.

Two hours of free parking are provided in the post office lot on a first come, first served basis.

Escape My Room does not allow walk-ins. Everyone visiting must have a ticket.

Tips + FAQ

  • Arrive 15 minutes before your scheduled time to have a chance to view the museum in the waiting room. Please do not arrive later than your scheduled ticket time or you may not be allowed entry into the room. We ask that you not arrive more than 30 minutes early as we may still be setting up the room from the previous visit.
  • Expect to be on our premises for up to 90 minutes. While you are only inside of the room for 1 hour, this includes time to view the museum and get acquainted with the story before entering the room.
  • Wear comfortable clothes. You may need to get on your hands and knees in order to solve a puzzle.
  • You may find it helpful to bring your reading glasses. Other than that, you won’t need any tools besides your working mind. We’ll require you to lock all personal belongings in a locker before entering the room.
  • Visibly intoxicated guests will not be permitted entry, and will not receive a refund. If you’ve had too much to drink, you may not have the ability to solve the puzzles.
  • Upon entering the room, you have exactly one hour.
Will I actually be locked in a room for an hour?

Yes, but there is a panic button if anyone is uncomfortable and wants to exit sooner. No re-entry into the room will be allowed for anyone who leaves, but guests who do not leave can continue playing the game.

Is the room scary?

No, Escape My Room is not meant to be scary, but it is mysterious. Your success depends on teamwork and problem-solving, NOT outrunning zombies or serial killers.

Is the room safe for kids?

Escape My Room can be fun for kids as long as there is adult supervision. We don’t recommend it for kids younger than 10, but all ages are allowed. There are no discounts for children. Well-behaved children over the age of 15 may not require adult supervision, but please write to us in advance.

Is the subject matter appropriate for kids?

If Escape My Room were a movie, we think it would get a PG rating. New Orleans is a city that has a dark history—slavery, prostitution, the mafia, and horrific disasters. Some of those topics may be brought up in the story of the rooms. If you have a specific concern, please write to us directly, and we’ll do our best to answer your question.

Will other people be in the room?

There are 7 tickets available for each time slot. If you are a group of less than 7 people, and do not want anyone else in the room with you, then you will need to book all 7 tickets.

How many people can the room hold? Can we bring 8 people?

The room holds 7 adults, and we don’t recommend bringing any additional people. If you absolutely must bring a 8th guest, you must have booked the entire room (all 7 tickets), and you will be able to pay for them when you arrive.

Will I have the room to myself/ourselves?

There are 7 tickets for each time slot available. If you want the room to yourself, you will need to book all 7 tickets or there is a chance that there will be other guests with you.

What is the minimum number of people you recommend?

Two people have successfully solved the room in under an hour, and one could probably solve it. That being said, we recommend at least 3 to 4 people minimum. If you are only booking two tickets, you might want to join another group or bring some friends.

Can I eat or drink in the rooms?

Unfortunately, we do not allow food or drinks into the rooms because the artifacts could get damaged if there were a spill. You are welcome to show up early and eat or drink in the waiting room. Water bottles are allowed.

Is the room handicapped accessible?

Yes, but it depends on the handicap and your group may require an extra docent or other participants to help. Please write to us at info@escapemyroom.com to discuss your specific situation before booking so we can make proper accommodations.

What if I don’t escape after an hour?

If you do not escape after one hour, an attendant will enter the room and show you the clues you missed. Only around 30% of groups escape on time, so don’t feel discouraged if you don’t escape.

Is photography allowed?

Photography is allowed and encouraged in the waiting room and DeLaporte Family Museum, but it is strictly prohibited in the escape rooms, where it could be damaging to the integrity of the puzzles and the privacy of the DeLaporte family.

What if I run late?

Escape My Room runs on a tight schedule which requires that participants arrive on time. You should arrive 15 minutes prior to the time on your ticket. You may not be allowed into the room if you show up later than your ticketed time.

Can I get a refund?

All ticket sales are final, and we generally do not give refunds for any reason. You are welcome to sell or give your ticket away to someone else.

Can I revisit the same room?

No, you cannot revisit the same room since you would already know how to solve it and escape. We plan on opening more rooms in the future, and if you sign up for our mailing list, you’ll be the first to hear about them.

Can I have a party at Escape My Room?

It depends on the group size and the time. The Mardi Gras study only holds 7 people at a time, so if your group is larger than 7, it would have to be split up into separate groups. Please write to us at info@escapemyroom.com with the date/time you are interested in and the total party size.

Is Escape My Room affiliated with the Federal Government?

No. Though located in an office building owned by the Federal Government, Escape My Room is in no way owned nor managed by the U.S. Postal Service. We are only tenants of the building.
