ESCAPE MY ROOM is an interactive game and museum in New Orleans, Louisiana. You and up to 6 guests are confined to a room for one hour. Your group's goal is to work together to solve puzzles centering around New Orleans' infamous family, the DeLaportes.

Can you escape?

You have one hour to break the chamber's codes and solve the family's mysteries. Prepare to be challenged! A great activity for friends, families, team building, or special events.

Rooms

The DeLaporte Mansion was demolished several years ago to make room for a new hospital complex near the New Orleans Superdome.

Before the house was torn down, everything within it was removed. A silent business partner has decided to make the family artifacts and heirlooms publicly available for the first time.

In each escape room you will uncover mysteries involving the secretive DeLaporte family and their hidden role in shaping New Orleans history.


The Mardi Gras Study

The year is 1990, and Odette DeLaporte has invited you to her study. As the last remaining heir in New Orleans to the DeLaporte fortune, she needs your help locating a treasure which went missing a long time ago. The reclusive matriarch has not been seen in public for quite some time, yet she has agreed to meet with you with the hope that you can recover whatâ€™s been lost.

The Mardi Gras study features family heirlooms dating back to the earliest Carnival parades and enchanting objects which hold generations of family secrets.

Tips + FAQs

  • Arrive 15 minutes early to have a chance to view the museum in the waiting room
  • Wear comfortable clothes. You may need to get on your hands and knees in order to solve a puzzle.
  • Bring nothing except a working mind. You won't need any tools, and we require you lock all personal belongings in a locker in the waiting room before entering the room.
  • If there are minors (under the age of 18) in your group, please make sure you bring a release form with their parents or guardians signature. Others will be required to sign a release form before entering.
  • Visibly intoxicated guests will not be permitted entry, and will not receive a refund. If you've had too much to drink, you may not have the ability to solve the puzzles.
  • Upon entering the room, you have exactly one hour.

Will I actually be locked in a room for an hour?

Yes, you and up to six guests will be locked in a room for an hour. There is a panic button if anyone is uncomfortable and wants to exit sooner. No re-entry into the room will be allowed.

Is Escape My Room scary?

No, Escape My Room is not meant to be scary, but it is mysterious. It is more a mental puzzle solving and teamwork challenge to escape.

Is the room safe for kids?

Escape My Room can be fun for kids as long as there is adult supervision. We do not recommend it for kids younger than 10, but all ages are permitted. For children under age 18, we require a liability form signed by a parent or guardian. There are no discounts for children. In certain cases, children under age 15 may not require adult supervision; please inquire in advance with our offices via email at info@escapemyroom.com.
Click here to digitally sign the liabiliy waiver form for minors.

Is the subject matter appropriate for kids?

If Escape My Room were a movie, we think it would receive a PG rating. New Orleans is a city that has a dark history—slavery, prostitution, the mafia, and natural disasters. Some of those topics may be mentioned in the story of the rooms. If you have a specific concern, please write us directly, and we'll do our best to answer your question.

How many people can the room hold? Can we bring 8 people?

The room holds 7 adults comfortably. We have had groups of 8 people come through, but it really depends on the size of the guests. Please write to us first if you have a group of 8. 8 people can also split up and do the room separately in two groups of 4.

Will other people be in the room?

There are 7 tickets available for each time slot. If you are a group of less than 7 people, and do not want anyone else in the room with you, then you will need to book all 7 tickets.

What if I don't escape after an hour?

If you do not escape after one hour, an attendant will enter the room and show you the clues you missed. Only around 30% of groups escape on time, so donâ€™t feel discouraged if you donâ€™t escape.

Is photography allowed?

Photography is allowed and encouraged in the waiting room, but not in the Mardi Gras Study in order to protect the integrity of the puzzles and the the privacy of the DeLaporte family.

What if I run late?

Escape My Room runs on a tight schedule which requires that participants arrive on time. Please plan to arrive up to 15 minutes early which will give you time to explore the artifacts in our unique waiting room. The time on your ticket is when you are supposed to be in the room solving puzzles, and it takes at least 5 minutes to give you an introduction and sign release waivers. If you are later than the time on your ticket, you will have less time inside of the room. Please call if you will arrive later than 10 minutes past the ticket time or you will forfeit your ticket and not be allowed into the room.

Can I get a refund?

All ticket sales are final, and we generally do not issue refunds. You are welcome to transfer your ticket to someone else, though rescheduling in not currently an option. If you make a mistake when you book the tickets, please let us know immediately so that we can help you fix the problem.

Can I revisit the same room?

No. You cannot revisit the same room, since you would already know how to solve its clues and escape. We are currently developing new escape rooms for the future. To stay updated, please subscribe to our mailing list. You'll be the first to hear about them.

Is the room handicapped accessible?

Yes, but it depends on the handicap and may require additional documentation or the help of accompanying participants. Please write us before booking to discuss your specific situation, so we may arrange proper accommodations for you or your group.

Can I eat or drink in the rooms?

Unfortunately, we do not allow food or drinks into the rooms. We require this of guests in order to protect the escape rooms and their artifacts from potential damage. You are welcome to show up early and eat or drink in the waiting room. Drinks with lids or water bottles are allowed.

Can I have a party at Escape My Room?

It depends on the group size and the time. The Mardi Gras study only holds 7 people at a time, so if your group is larger than 7, it would have to be split up into separate groups. Those not in the room could socialize in the waiting room and then swap out. Please write to us at info@escapemyroom.com with the date/time you are interested in and the total party size.

Is Escape My Room affiliated with the Federal Government?

No. Though located in an office building owned by the Federal Government, Escape My Room is in no way owned nor managed by the U.S. Postal Service. We are only tenants of the building.

Is the Delaporte family a real family?

Our lawyers make us write this disclaimer on each page: "All characters and entities appearing in this work are fictitious. Any resemblance to real persons, dead or alive, or other real-life entities, past or present, is purely coincidental."

In the words of one Nobel Prize-winning novelist, however, "fiction reveals truth that reality obscures."

Location

Escape My Room is located in the U.S. Postal Service building on Loyola Avenue, between Girod and Julia Streets, one block from the Superdome and on the Loyola-UPT Streetcar line. Our building is within walking distance or a short cab ride from most major hotels. Specific details about how to enter the building will be provided upon booking.

Two hours of free parking are provided in the adjacent lot.

Escape My Room does not allow walk-ins. Everyone visiting must have a ticket or security will not provide entry into the building.
