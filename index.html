How it works

1. Invite friends

Invite friends, family, or coworkers! Escape My Room is best experienced as part of a team. Each escape room can hold a maximum of 8 guests, but you can book as many or as few tickets as you like. If you don’t book all 8 tickets in your timeslot, there’s a good chance someone else will book the remaining tickets and you’ll make new friends!

2. Buy tickets

Buy tickets here on our website. All Escape My Room tickets must be purchased at least one hour in advance. Walk-ins are not allowed. Choose your room, select the number of tickets you’ll need, and then find a date and a time that work well for you.

3. Get locked in a room

Get locked in a room! You have one hour to escape, but if you do not escape within the hour, then we’ll come in and show you what you missed. Please arrive on time to be able to look around the family museum. You risk forfeiting your ticket if you are more than 15 minutes late.

4. Solve puzzles

While in the room, you must work together as a team to find clues and solve puzzles,uncovering secrets and scandals of the DeLaporte family along the way.

5. Come back

Come back! We debut a new escape room every two to four months, so If you have a good time (and we promise that you will), come back and play a different room!

Still have questions? Look at our Tips and FAQs or email us at info@escapemyroom.com

Historic Home Demolished for Hospital Construction

Despite efforts from the Preservation Resource Center and the Louisiana Landmarks Society, the DeLaporte home was demolished yesterday to make room for the new University Medical Center. The house stood for almost two centuries and was once part of a much larger estate that stretched from the edge of the French Quarter to Lake Pontchartrain.

Property records indicate that it is was owned by La Valliere LLC, a shell corporation registered in the Cayman Islands. This made it all but impossible to track down the current owners.

It was most recently inhabited by Mrs. Odette DeLaporte, who operated various family businesses after her husband passed away in the great storm of 1947. After her daughter vanished on Mardi Gras day in 1954, she became a recluse and was rarely seen.

Although the grounds continued to be maintained, the home slowly deteriorated and deemed a public hazardóits crumbling walls became a target for vandalizers and urban explorers. Deterioration was hastened when a fire destroyed part of the roof in early 2000.

In July of 2005, a group of determined urban explorers accessed the home, attempting to photograph and catalogue its interior. One of the explorers described the state of the ruins,

“It was of old brick, and dismal, and had a great many iron bars to it. Some of the windows had been walled up; of those that remained, all the lower were rustily barred. Despite being abandoned, It retained the appearance of order. Books still sat on the shelves, slippers by an armchair. It was like everything in the room had stopped, like the hands on a watch or a clock, a long time ago. We were so mesmerized by the bizarre collection of items that we barely had time to walk through the first floor before neighborhood patrol arrived and kicked us out. Weirdly, none of us even managed to snap a good photo. Our cards all malfunctioned and were empty! We hoped to return to take more pictures, but then it was too late.”

Hurricane Katrina hit New Orleans on August 29, 2005, and when neighbors slowly started returning to the neighborhood, they realized that the Delaporte Mansion had been emptied, presumably by looters.

“Thank goodness they didnít get in my house,” said Harvey Wellington, a long-time neighbor. “The only house looted on the whole block was the one nobody was living in. Lucky, I guess.”

The house was built in 1792 by Andree and Marguerite Delaporte when they were granted a plot of land from King Louis XIV of France. For their wealth, New Orleans society forgave the DeLaportes their eccentricity. The family lineage was not infrequently marred with scandal and infamy. Once New Orleans’ finest residence, the DeLaporte mansion and artifacts are now the stuff of legend.

TIPS / FAQ:

  • Walk-ins are not allowed, please purchase tickets online at least one hour in advance.
  • Arrive on time to view the family museum. Please do not arrive more than 15 minutes late as you may not be allowed entry.
  • Expect to be on our premises for up to 2 hours. While you are only inside of the room for 1 hour, this includes time to view the museum and get acquainted with the story before entering the room.
  • Visibly intoxicated guests will not be permitted entry, and will not receive a refund. If you’ve had too much to drink, you may not have the ability to solve the puzzles.
  • Escape My Room was not designed for children under the age of 12. Children under 12 are only allowed if your group has reserved all 8 tickets for the room.
  • You may find it helpful to bring your reading glasses. Other than that, bring nothing but a working mind.
  • Upon entering the room, you have exactly one hour to escape.
Will I actually be locked in a room for an hour?

Yes, but there is a panic button if anyone is uncomfortable and wants to exit sooner. No re-entry into the room will be allowed for anyone who leaves, but guests who do not leave can continue playing the game.

Is the room scary?

No, Escape My Room is not meant to be scary, but it is mysterious. It is more a mental puzzle solving and teamwork challenge to escape.

Is the room kid friendly?

Escape My Room was not designed for children younger than 12. Children under 12 are not allowed unless your group has purchased all 8 tickets for the room. It can still be fun for kids as long as there is adult supervision.

Is the subject matter appropriate for kids?

If Escape My Room was a movie, we think it would get a PG rating. New Orleans is a city that has a dark history—slavery, prostitution, the mafia, and horrific disasters. Some of those topics may be brought up in the story of the rooms. If you have a specific concern, please write to us directly, and we’ll do our best to answer your question.

Will other people be in the room?

There are 8 tickets available for each time slot. If you are a group of less than 8 people, and do not want anyone else in the room with you, then you will need to book all 8 tickets.

How many people can the room hold? Can we bring 9 people?

The room holds 8 adults, and we don’t recommend bringing any additional people. If you absolutely must bring a 9th guest, you must have booked the entire room (all 8 tickets), and you will be able to pay for them when you arrive.

Will I have the room to myself/ourselves?

There are 8 tickets for each time slot available. If you want the room to yourself, you will need to book all 8 tickets or there is a chance that there will be other guests with you.

What is the minimum number of people you recommend?

Two people have successfully solved the room in under an hour, and one could probably solve it. That being said, we recommend at least 3 to 4 people minimum. If you are only booking two tickets, you might want to join another group or bring some friends.

Can I eat or drink in the rooms?

Unfortunately, we do not allow food or drinks into the rooms because the artifacts could get damaged if there was a spill. You are welcome to show up early and eat or drink in the waiting room. Water bottles are allowed.

Is the room handicapped accessible?

Yes, but it depends on the handicap and your group may require an extra docent or other participants to help. Please write to us at info@escapemyroom.com to discuss your specific situation before booking so we can make proper accommodations.

What if I don’t escape after an hour?

If you do not escape after one hour, an attendant will enter the room and show you the clues you missed. Only around 30% of groups escape on time, so don’t feel discouraged if you don’t escape.

Is photography allowed?

Photography is allowed and encouraged in the waiting room, but not in the escape rooms in order to protect the integrity of the puzzles and the the privacy of the DeLaporte family.

What if I run late?

Escape My Room runs on a tight schedule which requires that participants arrive on time. The time on your ticket is when you should be walking in our front door. There is no need to arrive early, but if you are more than 15 minutes late you may forfeit your ticket and not be allowed into the room.

Can I get a refund?

All ticket sales are final, and we generally do not give refunds for any reason. You are welcome to sell or give your ticket away to someone else.

Can I revisit the same room?

No, you cannot revisit the same room since you would already know how to solve it and escape. We plan on opening more rooms in the future, and if you sign up for our mailing list, you’ll be the first to hear about them.

Can I have a party at Escape My Room?

Please write to us at info@escapemyroom.com with the date/time you are interested in and the total party size.
