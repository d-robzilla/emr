Press Releases

June 9, 2015 — Tickets go on sale through July 31, 2015

June 9, 2015

New Orleans, LA —

Escape My Room opens to the public on June 17, 2015.

Escape My Room is an interactive game and museum in New Orleans, Louisiana. Up to 7 players are locked in a room for one hour. Players must work together to discover clues and solve puzzles in order to escape before the time is up. The game is great for friends, families, corporate team building, or special events.

Escape rooms are a new genre of entertainment and tourist attraction which have spread around the world since first becoming popular in Japan in 2013. The real world escape room games were originally based on digital video games.

Escape My Room in New Orleans will grow to form a collection of various escape rooms, all narratively centered around an eccentric New Orleans family whose home was demolished to make room for the new hospital complex in Mid-City. A silent partner has made these artifacts available for the first time.

The Mardi Gras Study will open to the public for the first time on June 17, and it follows the story of Odette DeLaporte—a reclusive matriarch who needs your help recovering a treasure which went missing a long time ago. The room features heirlooms dating back to the earliest Carnival parades and enchanting objects which hold generations of family secrets.

Timed entry tickets can be purchased online at www.escapemyroom.com for $28 a person (starting June 9). Tickets grant you entry to the family museum/waiting room, and the Mardi Gras Study escape room.

Media previews available from June 9 until June 17. Please contact Andrew Preble at apreble@escapemyroom.com or 985-869-2343 to reserve a time slot or schedule an interview. Accredited media can test the escape room free of charge, but a group of at least 5 players is recommended. We can also arrange for a test group to play the game and be interviewed on camera afterwards.

Location details:

Escape My Room is located at 701 Loyola Avenue in the U.S. Postal Service building between Girod and Julia Streets, one block from the Superdome and on the Loyola-UPT Streetcar line. Our building is within walking distance or a short cab ride from most major hotels. Specific details about how to enter the building will be provided upon booking. Two hours of free parking are provided in the adjacent lot. Escape My Room does not allow walk-ins. Everyone visiting must have a ticket or security will not provide entry into the building.

Quotes

Quotes from Andrew Preble

“As a kid, my mom designed elaborate scavenger hunts which had me and my friends wandering around the whole town. Escape My Room is like a scavenger hunt, but you’re confined to one room and have only an hour to solve it.”

“When I first got the idea to build Escape My Room, I visited several around the country. I was disappointed that there was little to no story. They were basically rooms with IKEA furniture and a bunch of locked boxes. Find the key, and open the box.”

“With Escape My Room, we wanted to create a deeper story, and we wanted guests to learn something along the way.”

“In the Mardi Gras Study we are telling a story that we hope to continue on into future rooms. The next room will be a music room, centered around a creole man that was also a Mardi Gras Indian.”

Quotes from Michelle Calabro

"I was working in marketing at the time, for a company that comes up with experiential marketing campaigns. What I loved the most about those projects was how they drew upon every aspect of my past experiences: photography, storytelling, new tech trends, to create new kinds of experiences for people, on behalf of brands. When Andrew read the article and told me he wanted to make an escape room, I saw it as an opportunity to actually produce the kinds of experiences I had been brainstorming in the past."

"An Experience Designer is someone who designs the intellectual and emotional interactions that people have with each other, at events, at theater performances, while using websites, while interacting with media art, etc. There's usually a designed artifact or set of artifacts that help to create 'Experiences,' but sometimes designed experiences are ephemeral, game-like and rule-based."

"My primary goal in designing Escape My Room was to create a fun, unique experience. Over the course of designing the first room, we had to decide whether to keep certain details in the story, and eventually chose fun over detail. The kind of fun that people have at EMR is sort of frantic and a little anxious, because they're racing against the clock to escape."

"I've made artwork that deals with secrets, love, communication and conflict resolution, and I see those themes in the Escape Room's story, and in how team members work together to escape. It's fascinating for me to watch, because each team is different."
